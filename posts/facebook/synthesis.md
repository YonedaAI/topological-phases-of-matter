🧵 Five research papers, each proving something real on its own. The sixth ties them into one sequence.

Here it is: local quantum interactions become a condensed moduli stack of Hamiltonians, which contains a uniformly gapped substack, which stabilizes into a phase infinity-groupoid, which group-completes into an invertible phase spectrum. Five arrows, five papers, each one a genuine module.

Part I builds the ground floor: the space of interactions as a rigorous mathematical object with a light cone that stays uniform. Part II attaches the observable algebra and, through a bridge called solidification, connects it to the K-theory invariants physicists already use. Part III proves the gapped region is stable exactly where standard physical conditions hold, while being honest that a hard theorem (Cubitt-Pérez-García-Wolf) rules out ever deciding this in general. Part IV shows that stacking phases together, then completing the invertible ones, produces a genuine spectrum, and compares it to the field-theory classification as an open question. Part V asks which of those abstract classes a real lattice model can build, and proves a hard limit: some phases, chiral ones like Chern insulators, can never come from that entire class of models.

Why it matters: this was never about finding a new invariant, a new number nobody had computed before. It is about giving all of these separate ideas, families of Hamiltonians, disorder, K-theory, stacking, phase transitions, one shared mathematical home. Every module is backed by real, runnable checks: 72 automated property tests, 198 deterministic checks, and a machine-verified Lean 4 proof library covering all five component problems, with zero unproven gaps left in it.
