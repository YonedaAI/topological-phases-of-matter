🏗️ Suppose mathematicians hand you a complete abstract classification of topological phases. The question a physicist actually cares about next: can you build one? With an explicit lattice model, not just an abstract symbol?

That is what Part V of our series is about: realizability. And it comes with genuinely good news and a genuinely hard limit.

The good news: every class coming from group cohomology, a large and important family, can be built explicitly, using commuting-projector models (a kind of exactly-solvable lattice Hamiltonian) with exactly the right boundary behavior. In one and two dimensions this classification is provably complete. Nothing is missing there.

The hard limit: a theorem by Kapustin and Fidkowski proves that no commuting-projector model can ever realize a chiral phase, the category that includes real materials like Chern insulators and fractional quantum Hall states. This is not a gap in current technique. It is a proof that this entire model-building strategy is the wrong tool for that job, permanently.

Why it matters: instead of quietly avoiding this obstruction, we build it directly into the theory, and lay out, as open conjectures, what a chiral phase would need instead (non-commuting gapped models, which do exist physically).
