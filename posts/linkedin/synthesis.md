A six-paper research program needs one paper that shows the pieces fit together, not just that each piece works on its own. Part VI is that paper, built around a single sequence: local quantum interactions, then the condensed moduli stack of Hamiltonians, then its uniformly gapped substack, then the stabilized phase infinity-groupoid, then the invertible condensed phase spectrum. Each arrow in that sequence is owned by one of the preceding five papers.

We treat the whole thing as a modular composition on purpose. Part I turns the interaction space into a condensed Banach object with a uniform Lieb-Robinson bound. Part II attaches the observable algebra and, via Aoki's solidification theorem, a solid K-theory invariant. Part III cuts out the stable, uniformly gapped locus against the wall of Cubitt-Pérez-García-Wolf undecidability. Part IV group-completes the invertible sector into a genuine spectrum and sets it against the Freed-Hopkins field-theory classification. Part V asks which of those classes an explicit lattice model can realize, bounded by the Kapustin-Fidkowski no-go. Each module takes the previous module's output as its input, and new structure appears at every stage, including functorial invariants of disordered families and an obstruction subgroup separating abstract classes from buildable ones.

What we prove is module-level: nineteen conjectures across the five papers, cited and numbered. Above that sit four program-wide conjectures we state and do not claim to have proved, including the Master Conjecture that all five modules glue into one condensed higher stack. The whole series runs on 72 property-based QuickCheck tests, 198 deterministic checks, and a sorry-free Lean 4 library formalizing all five component problems. The thesis was never a different Chern number; it is one categorical and homological environment for continuous families, disorder, K-theory, stacking, and transition charges.
