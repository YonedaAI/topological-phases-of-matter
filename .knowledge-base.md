## 1. Program overview (faithful summary of `context/source.txt`)

### 1.1 The central proposal
Fix a spatial dimension `d`, a lattice / coarse metric space `L`, local Hilbert spaces, a locality (interaction-decay) condition, and a symmetry group `G`. Let `𝓘_{d,G}` be the topological space of admissible `G`-symmetric local/quasi-local interactions. Instead of using it as a bare topological space, pass to its **condensation**
> `X ↦ 𝑋̲`, with `𝑋̲(S) = Cont(S, X)`, `S` ranging over profinite sets.

A condensed set is a sheaf on the site of profinite sets with finite jointly-surjective covers. For compactly generated spaces `X ↦ 𝑋̲` is **fully faithful**, so ordinary coupling spaces, tori, Banach spaces, and compact disorder spaces embed into a category with better algebraic/homological behavior (condensed abelian groups form a Grothendieck abelian category). The correct object is generally a **condensed anima / stack**, since Hamiltonians carry automorphisms, gauge and higher equivalences.

### 1.2 The condensed gapped moduli stack
For a profinite test object `S`:
- `𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}(S)` = `S`-continuous families of `G`-symmetric local/quasi-local quantum interactions.
- **Uniformly gapped substack:** `𝕲𝖆𝖕_{d,G}(S) = ⋃_{Δ>0} { H ∈ 𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}(S) : inf_{s∈S} gap(H_s) ≥ Δ }`. The word **uniformly** is essential: a family with `inf_s gap(H_s)=0` is not a safely gapped family.
- **Stabilized phase ∞-groupoid:** `𝕻𝖍𝖆𝖘𝖊_{d,G} := ( 𝕲𝖆𝖕_{d,G}[𝓦^{-1}] )^{st}`, where `𝓦` contains gapped adiabatic / quasi-local (finite-depth) equivalences to be inverted, and `(−)^{st}` stabilizes by trivial product-state ancillas.
- **Phases:** `Phases_{d,G} = π₀ Shape( 𝕻𝖍𝖆𝖘𝖊_{d,G} )`.

> **Boxed slogan.** topological phase = a component of the stabilized condensed stack of gapped systems.

**`𝓦` is part of the definition** of the physical phase problem: isomorphism, gapped homotopy, stable equivalence, and `K`-theoretic equivalence need not coincide. Quasi-adiabatic continuation is the bridge between uniformly gapped paths and quasi-local transformations of observables.

### 1.3 Why the whole homotopy type matters
`π₀` records which phases exist. Higher homotopy carries more physics:
- `π₁(𝕻𝖍𝖆𝖘𝖊)` = loops of gapped Hamiltonians = adiabatic pumps and automorphisms of topological order;
- `π_n` = `n`-parameter families, higher pumps, families of defects.

Stacking gives `⊠ : 𝕻𝖍𝖆𝖘𝖊 × 𝕻𝖍𝖆𝖘𝖊 → 𝕻𝖍𝖆𝖘𝖊`, a symmetric-monoidal / `E∞` structure. Restricting to invertible phases and group-completing yields a connective condensed spectrum `𝐈𝐏^{cond}_{d,G}` (the condensed analogue of the spectrum-of-phases viewpoint). Symmetry is a condensed group `𝐺̲`; symmetric systems use strict or homotopy fixed points `𝕻𝖍𝖆𝖘𝖊_d^{h𝐺̲}`; spatial symmetry uses an action stack `[Y/𝐺̲]`.

### 1.4 Phase transitions as the gapless discriminant
For a parameter space `B` (couplings, fields, pressures, disorder, boundary conditions), condense to `𝐵̲` and take a family `f : 𝐵̲ → 𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}`. Define
- gapped locus `U_f = 𝐵̲ ×_{𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}} 𝕲𝖆𝖕_{d,G}`, gapless discriminant `Σ_f = 𝐵̲ ∖ U_f`;
- phase label `ν_f : U_f → π₀(𝕻𝖍𝖆𝖘𝖊_{d,G})`, locally constant on `U_f`.

A topological phase transition is a path in `B` whose endpoints carry different `ν_f`; it must cross `Σ_f`.
> **Boxed slogan.** topological transition = crossing the gapless discriminant in the Hamiltonian moduli stack.

If the invariant lives in a generalized cohomology theory `E`, so `ν ∈ E^q(U_f)`, the obstruction to extending it across the critical set is the **relative transition charge** `∂ν ∈ E^{q+1}(B, U_f)`. Locally, a small sphere linking a component of `Σ_f` detects the jump (Dirac/Weyl node as source/sink of topological charge), but now for generalized cohomology and interacting phase spectra.

### 1.5 SSH worked example (source §5)
`H(k;t₁,t₂) = (t₁ + t₂ cos k) σ_x + t₂ sin k σ_y`, `q(k) = t₁ + t₂ e^{ik}`, `E_±(k) = ±|q(k)|`. Gapped iff `|t₁| ≠ |t₂|`; discriminant `Σ = {|t₁| = |t₂|}`. Winding number of `q : S¹ → ℂˣ` is `ν = 1` for `|t₁|<|t₂|`, `ν = 0` for `|t₁|>|t₂|` (up to orientation). In the condensed formulation replace `B`, `S¹` by `𝐵̲`, `𝑆¹̲` and view the family as a morphism into the condensed gapped moduli stack; the number is unchanged, but it becomes part of a functorial theory of families, descent, completions, symmetries. Free-fermion band systems are the best-developed setting.

### 1.6 The strongest concrete bridge: solid K-theory (source §6)
For a topological algebra `A`, `𝐴̲(S) = C(S, A)`. **Aoki's theorem** (arXiv:2409.01462): solidification of the algebraic `K`-theory of such condensed algebras recovers semitopological `K`-theory, and for (real) Banach algebras the topological/operator `K`-theory:
> `K_op(A) ≃ Solid( K_alg(𝐴̲) )` (with connective / Bott-periodic qualifications).
Route: Hamiltonian ↦ condensed observable algebra ↦ `K_alg(𝐴̲)` ↦ `Solid(K_alg(𝐴̲))` ↦ operator-`K` phase invariant.

### 1.7 Disorder and aperiodicity (source §7)
For a finite local-configuration set `F`, `Ω = F^{ℤ^d}` is compact totally disconnected, i.e. **profinite**. A disordered family `ω ↦ H_ω` is literally an `Ω`-point: `H ∈ 𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}(Ω)`. Finite quotients of `Ω` are finite-resolution disorder data; the sheaf condition expresses compatibility across finite approximations. Natural for: disorder-robust labels, quasicrystalline/substitution hulls, inverse limits of finite-volume parameter spaces, crossed-product observable algebras, profinitely varying symmetry data. Here condensed probes *match the physical configuration space* rather than repackage a smooth manifold.

### 1.8 What the paradigm contributes — and what it does not (source §8)
Contributes: (1) one category for continuous families, profinite disorder, inverse limits, topological symmetry; (2) exact derived operations (condensed abelian groups / solid modules / solid spectra) where topological groups behave badly; (3) sheaf/stack organization of locality, defects, interfaces, boundaries, symmetry, gluing. Does **not** by itself solve the hard analysis. One must still establish:
1. locality and Lieb–Robinson-type estimates **(Paper I)**;
2. positivity and `C*`-norm conditions **(Paper II)**;
3. existence and stability of a thermodynamic spectral gap **(Paper III)**;
4. equivalence between microscopic lattice systems and effective field theories **(Paper IV)**;
5. physical realizability of abstract bordism/homotopy classes **(Paper V)**.
Noninvertible topological order needs higher categories (braided/modular tensor categories in 2+1D and higher analogues); a full condensed enhancement would need a condensed higher stack of phases and defects, not just a `K`-theory spectrum. And **not every abstract invertible field theory is known to arise from a microscopic gapped system** (§8, sharpened in Paper V).

### 1.9 The dictionary (quote verbatim)
```
local quantum interactions
  ↓
condensed moduli stack of Hamiltonians
  ↓
uniformly gapped substack
  ↓
stabilized phase ∞-groupoid
  ↓
invertible condensed phase spectrum
```
`π₀` = phases · `π₁` = adiabatic pumps and phase automorphisms · `π_n` = higher families and higher defects · `Σ` = gapless transition locus · `E^{q+1}(B, B∖Σ)` = relative charge of a transition.

**The genuinely new thesis** is not a different Chern number; it is a *single categorical and homological environment* in which continuous families, profinite disorder, analytic completions, operator `K`-theory, symmetry, stacking, defects, and transition loci are treated simultaneously.

## 2. Paper I — `lieb-robinson-locality`
### Locality and Lieb–Robinson estimates; condensed structure on interaction spaces
*Companion key:* `paperLocality`. *Program input #1 (§1.8).*

**(a) Scope & place.** Provide the analytic backbone: the interaction space `𝓘_{d,G}` is normed by an `F`-function locality norm making it a Banach space `𝓑_F`; its condensation `𝓑_F̲(S)=Cont(S,𝓑_F)` is the ground floor of `𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}`. Lieb–Robinson (LR) bounds control the Heisenberg dynamics and, crucially, are **continuous in the interaction and the parameter**, which is exactly what is needed to promote "a family of dynamics" to a morphism of condensed objects and to give meaning to `S`-continuous families.

**(b) Known results (cited).**
- LR finite group velocity for spin systems: `lieb-robinson-1972`. Modern general formulations with `F`-functions and quasi-local maps: `nsy-quasilocality-1` (Part I) and `nsy-quasilocality-2` (Part II); pedagogical: `hastings-locality`.
- Gap ⇒ exponential clustering (LR ⇒ correlation decay): `nachtergaele-sims-clustering`, `hastings-koma`.
- Quasi-adiabatic continuation / spectral flow: `hastings-wen-qac`; automorphic equivalence of gapped phases via quasi-local flows: `bmns-automorphic` (this is the rigorous meaning of `𝓦` for adiabatically connected gapped systems).
- Condensation `X ↦ 𝑋̲` fully faithful on compactly generated spaces; condensed abelian groups a Grothendieck abelian category: `clausen-scholze-condensed`, `barwick-haine-pyknotic`.

**(c) Provable today — candidate Theorems.**
- **Thm I-A (Banach interaction space).** For a fixed `F`-function, the `G`-symmetric interactions with finite `F`-norm form a (real/complex) Banach space `𝓑_F`; the Heisenberg dynamics `τ_t` is a strongly continuous family of `*`-automorphisms of the quasi-local algebra with LR bound. *(Repackaging `nsy-quasilocality-1`; genuinely provable.)*
- **Thm I-B (Condensed dynamics).** Because `𝓑_F` is a Banach space, `𝓑_F̲` is a condensed `ℝ`-vector space, and by full faithfulness of `(−)̲` on compactly generated spaces the LR-continuous assignment `Φ ↦ τ_t^Φ` defines a morphism of condensed sets `𝓑_F̲ → Aut̲(𝒜)`. *(Combine `clausen-scholze-condensed` + `nsy-quasilocality-1`.)*
- **Thm I-C (Profinite family = compatible finite data).** For profinite `S = lim S_i`, an `S`-continuous family of interactions is the same as a compatible system of `S_i`-families (sheaf/`lim` description), because `Cont(lim S_i, 𝓑_F) = colim Cont(S_i,𝓑_F)`-completion; LR bounds pass to the limit uniformly. *(Standard condensed limit + uniform LR.)*

**(d) Open — numbered Conjectures.**
- **Conjecture I-1.** `𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}` is a **condensed (higher) stack** (not merely a condensed set): the groupoid of gauge/quasi-local automorphisms glues along finite covers of profinite probes.
- **Conjecture I-2.** The quasi-local automorphisms generated by time-dependent `F`-function interactions form a **condensed group** acting on `𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}`, and quasi-adiabatic continuation is an internal path-lifting for this action.
- **Conjecture I-3 (descent).** LR-type estimates satisfy descent along finite quotients of a disorder hull `Ω` (§1.7), so quasi-local invariants are determined by finite-resolution data.

**(e) Key techniques.** `F`-functions and reweighting; LR series / Dyson expansion; quasi-local maps and spectral-flow (`τ`-)automorphisms (`nsy-quasilocality-1`); Araki-type analyticity; condensation of Banach spaces and internal-hom in condensed sets.

**(f) Pitfalls & bounds.** Quasi-local ≠ strictly local — keep the `F`-norm explicit. The stack structure (Conj I-1) is *not* yet a theorem; do not assert it. "Continuity in the interaction" must be stated in the `𝓑_F` topology, not a weaker one, or Thm I-B fails. Long-range interactions can violate standard LR; restrict to the `F`-function class.

## 3. Paper II — `positivity-cstar-norms`
### Positivity, C*-norm conditions, condensed state spaces, and the solid K-theory bridge
*Companion key:* `paperPositivity`. *Program input #2.*

**(a) Scope & place.** Turn the observable side into condensed data: the quasi-local `C*`-algebra `𝒜`, its **compact** state space `𝒮(𝒜)` (weak-`*`), and the condensed observable algebra `𝐴̲(S)=C(S,A)`. The payoff is Aoki's bridge: an operator-`K` phase invariant arises from an intrinsically condensed operation (solidification of algebraic `K`-theory). Also the natural home for **disorder** via crossed products.

**(b) Known results (cited).**
- **Aoki's theorem** (the load-bearing bridge): `aoki-solidification` — solidification of `K_alg` of condensed (real) algebras recovers semitopological `K`-theory, and for real **Banach** algebras the topological/operator `K`-theory. This is the rigorous content behind §1.6.
- Operator-`K`/`C*`-algebraic classification of free-fermion / disordered phases: `thiang-ktheory` (model-independent `C*`-superalgebra), `bellissard-ncg-qhe` (NCG of QHE, crossed products `C(Ω) ⋊ ℤ^d`), `prodan-sb` (bulk & boundary invariants, KKO for anti-linear symmetries), `bourne-kellendonk-rennie` (KK bulk-edge), `kubota-controlled` (Roe/coarse `K`-theory).
- Disorder hull `C*`-algebras & gap labelling: `kellendonk-tilings`.
- Condensed/solid foundations: `clausen-scholze-condensed`, `clausen-scholze-analytic`, `clausen-scholze-analytic-stacks`.

**(c) Provable today — candidate Theorems.**
- **Thm II-A (Condensed state space).** For unital `C*`-algebra `A`, `𝒮(A)` is compact Hausdorff, so `𝒮(A)̲` is a compact-Hausdorff condensed set and `(−)̲` is fully faithful on it; positivity is a **closed** condition, cutting out a closed condensed subobject of the condensed dual. *(CS full faithfulness + Banach–Alaoglu.)*
- **Thm II-B (Solid K-theory invariant).** For the (real) Banach observable algebra `A` of a free-fermion/disordered system, `K_op(A) ≃ Solid(K_alg(𝐴̲))` as spectra (with the connective/Bott qualifications). Hence the standard topological-insulator invariants are values of a condensed-mathematical functor. *(Direct application of `aoki-solidification`; cite the qualifications exactly.)*
- **Thm II-C (Crossed-product disorder algebra).** For a disorder hull `Ω` (§1.7) with `ℤ^d`-action, `C(Ω) ⋊ ℤ^d` is a `C*`-algebra whose `K`-theory computes the disorder-averaged invariants (Bellissard); its condensation is functorial in finite quotients of `Ω`. *(Repackaging `bellissard-ncg-qhe` + condensation.)*

**(d) Open — numbered Conjectures.**
- **Conjecture II-1.** The assignment `H ↦ (𝒜, 𝒮(𝒜))` upgrades to a morphism of condensed stacks `𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G} → (condensed C*-algebras with state space)`, and positivity + the `C*`-identity cut out `𝕳𝖆𝖒` as a closed condensed substack of a larger "formal-interaction" stack.
- **Conjecture II-2 (functorial solid invariant).** For a disordered family `H ∈ 𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}(Ω)`, the solid-`K` invariant of §1.6 equals the operator-`K` invariant of the crossed product, **naturally in `Ω`** (compatible with all finite quotients).
- **Conjecture II-3.** Real/`KKO` subtleties (time-reversal, particle–hole) are captured by solidification of a real/Real algebraic `K`-theory, matching the Kitaev periodic table `KO`-degrees.

**(e) Key techniques.** GNS; Bratteli–Robinson `C*`-machinery; weak-`*` compactness; crossed products and the Pimsner–Voiculescu / Bellissard gap-labelling; solid modules and solidification (`clausen-scholze-analytic`); real `K`-theory / `KKO`.

**(f) Pitfalls & bounds.** State Aoki's **qualifiers** (connective vs periodic; real vs complex) — do not cite `K_op ≃ Solid(K_alg(𝐴̲))` without them. Nonunital algebras need care for `𝒮(A)` (multiplier/unitization). Light-condensed vs full-condensed set-theoretic issues: prefer light condensed (`clausen-scholze-analytic-stacks`) to avoid size problems in the disorder inverse limits. Solid `K`-theory recovers *invertible/free-fermion* invariants; noninvertible order is out of scope here (needs Paper V's higher-categorical caveat).

## 4. Paper III — `spectral-gap-stability`
### The uniformly gapped substack: existence and stability of the thermodynamic spectral gap
*Companion key:* `paperGap`. *Program input #3. This is the most delicate paper — see the CPW pitfall.*

**(a) Scope & place.** Justify that `𝕲𝖆𝖕_{d,G} ⊂ 𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}` is a well-behaved (ideally **open**) condensed substack, and that membership is **stable** under small quasi-local perturbations — the property that makes `π₀` of the localized/stabilized stack a robust invariant. The whole phase functor is only as good as this substack.

**(b) Known results (cited).**
- Stability of the (bulk) gap under local perturbations for topologically ordered / frustration-free systems: `bhm-stability`, `bravyi-hastings-shortproof`, `michalakis-zwolak` (LTQO ⇔ stability + area law), `nsy-bulkgap` (infinite-volume GNS version, no uniform finite-size lower bound assumed).
- Gap ⇒ clustering (structural consequence of a gap): `hastings-koma`, `nachtergaele-sims-clustering`.
- Adiabatic/automorphic equivalence within a gapped phase: `bmns-automorphic`, `hastings-wen-qac`.
- **Hard limit:** undecidability of the spectral gap: `cpw-undecidable` (Nature 2015).

**(c) Provable today — candidate Theorems.**
- **Thm III-A (Local continuity of the gap).** For a fixed finite volume, `gap(H)` is Lipschitz in the interaction (Weyl inequalities); hence on any *finite* quotient the gapped locus is open. *(Elementary; safe.)*
- **Thm III-B (Stability ⇒ open substack, conditionally).** Under LTQO + frustration-free hypotheses, the BHM/MZ/NSY theorems give a perturbation threshold `ε>0` independent of system size; consequently the corresponding sub-family of `𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}` (those satisfying the hypotheses with uniform constants) contains an `𝓑_F`-norm ball around each member inside `𝕲𝖆𝖕_{d,G}`. So on the *frustration-free/LTQO stratum*, `𝕲𝖆𝖕` is open and stable. *(Cite `bhm-stability`, `michalakis-zwolak`, `nsy-bulkgap`; the hypotheses are mandatory.)*
- **Thm III-C (Gap ⇒ clustering, uniformly).** A uniform gap `Δ` on a family yields exponential clustering with rate controlled by `Δ` and the LR velocity, uniformly over the profinite base. *(From `hastings-koma`/`nachtergaele-sims-clustering` + Paper I uniformity.)*

**(d) Open — numbered Conjectures.**
- **Conjecture III-1.** `𝕲𝖆𝖕_{d,G}` is an **open** condensed substack of `𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}` on the physically relevant stratum, and the "uniformly gapped" sheaf condition is detected by finite quotients of profinite probes.
- **Conjecture III-2 (uniform gap descent).** A family over profinite `Ω` is uniformly gapped iff each finite-resolution approximation is gapped with a common `Δ`; equivalently, uniform gappedness is a closed condition on the inverse system.
- **Conjecture III-3.** Quasi-adiabatic continuation defines, on `𝕲𝖆𝖕_{d,G}`, the internal path components realizing `𝓦`, so `π₀ Shape(𝕲𝖆𝖕[𝓦^{-1}])` equals the operator-algebraic set of gapped phases (`ogata-classification-review`).

**(e) Key techniques.** Bravyi–Hastings–Michalakis relative-form / spectral-flow bootstrap; LTQO indistinguishability radius; martingale/telescoping over length scales; GNS infinite-volume ground states (`nsy-bulkgap`); Weyl perturbation theory at finite volume.

**(f) Pitfalls & bounds (READ BEFORE WRITING).**
- **CPW undecidability (`cpw-undecidable`) is a hard wall.** There is **no** general algorithm deciding whether a translation-invariant nearest-neighbour family is gapped or gapless. Therefore:
  - Do **not** claim a general/uniform criterion, decision procedure, or lower bound for the gap of arbitrary members of `𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}`.
  - "Existence of a thermodynamic gap" is, in general, an **assumption defining the substack** `𝕲𝖆𝖕`, not a theorem. Frame Paper III around **stability** (perturbative, under stated LTQO/frustration-free hypotheses) and around the *structure* of the gapped substack, not around deciding gappedness.
  - Any openness/descent statement (Conj III-1/2) is claimed **on the stratum where the stability hypotheses hold with uniform constants**, and must say so.
- Frustration-free is a real restriction; general gapped systems need not be frustration-free. Don't silently extend frustration-free theorems.
- "Uniform" over the base is the crux (§1.2): a pointwise gap that degenerates is exactly what `𝕲𝖆𝖕` excludes; keep the `inf_s` explicit.

## 5. Paper IV — `lattice-eft-equivalence`
### From lattice models to effective field theories: stabilization and the invertible condensed phase spectrum
*Companion key:* `paperEFT`. *Program input #4. Builds the top of the tower: `𝐈𝐏^{cond}_{d,G}`.*

**(a) Scope & place.** Group-complete the invertible sector of `𝕻𝖍𝖆𝖘𝖊_{d,G}` to a connective condensed spectrum `𝐈𝐏^{cond}_{d,G}` and compare lattice invariants to effective-field-theory (EFT) classifications (bordism/stable homotopy). "Equivalence" = the invariant computed microscopically on the lattice agrees with the invertible-TQFT deformation class.

**(b) Known results (cited).**
- EFT/bordism classification of invertible/SPT phases (under EFT assumptions): `freed-hopkins` (reflection positivity ⇒ SPT via Thom spectra), `freed-sre` (SRE ⇒ invertible TQFT ⇒ stable homotopy), `kapustin-cobordism` (beyond group cohomology), `gaiotto-jf` and `xiong-minimalist` (generalized-cohomology/`Ω`-spectrum hypothesis), `cglw-cohomology` (group-cohomology SPT).
- Kitaev's spectrum proposal and its rigorous operator-algebraic realization: `kitaev-periodic-table`, and especially `kubota-omega-spectrum` (constructs an `Ω`-spectrum `IP*` whose homotopy groups are the invertible gapped spin systems — the closest existing theorem to `𝐈𝐏^{cond}`); homotopical foundations of parametrized systems: `beaudry-etal`.
- Rigorous lattice → invariant maps (the "microscopic" side): `kapustin-sopenko-noether` (higher Berry class; unifies Hall conductance and Thouless pump), `kapustin-sopenko-hall` (Hall conductance locally computable, integer-quantized for SRE, a phase invariant), `thouless-pump`, `tknn-1982`.
- Moduli-space-of-gapped-Hamiltonians precedent (ordinary topology + EFT): `hsin-wang-moduli`.

**(c) Provable today — candidate Theorems.**
- **Thm IV-A (Invertible sector is a Picard groupoid ⇒ spectrum).** The invertible objects of the symmetric-monoidal `(𝕻𝖍𝖆𝖘𝖊_{d,G}, ⊠)` form a Picard `∞`-groupoid; its group completion is a connective spectrum. *(Formal once `⊠` is `E∞`; `beaudry-etal`/`kubota-omega-spectrum` make instances rigorous.)*
- **Thm IV-B (Existence of the target spectrum).** `kubota-omega-spectrum` constructs an `Ω`-spectrum of invertible gapped spin systems (with crystallographic-symmetry variants) — a rigorous carrier for the homotopy groups `𝐈𝐏^{cond}` should have. *(Cited theorem; `𝐈𝐏^{cond}` is conjecturally its condensed refinement.)*
- **Thm IV-C (A rigorous lattice invariant).** The Hall conductance of a 2D SRE lattice state is locally computable, an integer multiple of `e²/h`, and constant on gapped phases (`kapustin-sopenko-hall`); the higher Berry class generalizes this to families (`kapustin-sopenko-noether`). *(These are the concrete lattice→`E`-cohomology maps `ν` of §1.4.)*

**(d) Open — numbered Conjectures.**
- **Conjecture IV-1 (condensed = solid refinement).** `𝐈𝐏^{cond}_{d,G}` is a connective **condensed/solid** spectrum whose underlying spectrum is (a suitable connective cover of) Kubota's `IP*` / the Freed–Hopkins bordism target after realification.
- **Conjecture IV-2 (lattice–EFT equivalence).** For SRE phases under EFT assumptions, the microscopic invariant (`ν ∈ E^q`, e.g. Hall/higher-Berry class) equals the deformation class of the associated invertible TQFT (`freed-hopkins`). The SSH class matches its EFT (`ssh-1979`).
- **Conjecture IV-3 (relative charge = spectral boundary map).** The transition charge `∂ν ∈ E^{q+1}(B,U_f)` is the boundary map of the cofiber sequence for `𝐈𝐏^{cond}`, computing the jump across `Σ_f`.

**(e) Key techniques.** Group completion / Picard spectra; bordism & Thom spectra (Freed–Hopkins); Anderson duality; parametrized stable homotopy (`beaudry-etal`); localization flow / coarse index (`kubota-omega-spectrum`); locally-computable index densities (`kapustin-sopenko-*`).

**(f) Pitfalls & bounds.** EFT classifications are proven **under EFT assumptions** — state them; a lattice system is not automatically an invertible TQFT (that is the SRE hypothesis). Stabilization by ancillas can **collapse** distinctions (that is intended — `π₀` of the stabilized stack — but say so). `𝐈𝐏^{cond}` as a *condensed* spectrum is conjectural (Conj IV-1); Kubota's is a *smooth/topological* `Ω`-spectrum, not yet condensed. Noninvertible order is explicitly out of scope (needs higher categories, §1.8).

## 6. Paper V — `bordism-realizability`
### Physical realizability of bordism and homotopy classes by gapped lattice systems
*Companion key:* `paperRealizability`. *Program input #5. Constrained by the Kapustin–Fidkowski no-go.*

**(a) Scope & place.** Address the gap between abstract classifications (bordism/homotopy, Paper IV) and explicit local lattice realizations: which classes in `𝐈𝐏^{cond}_{d,G}` are actually realized by a uniformly gapped lattice family? This is the surjectivity of `{lattice models}/𝓦 → {abstract classes}` onto a physically-realizable sub-spectrum (§1.8: "not every abstract invertible field theory is known to arise from a microscopic gapped system").

**(b) Known results (cited).**
- **Positive realizability (constructive):** group-cohomology SPT phases are realized by explicit models: `cglw-cohomology`; edge/anomaly characterization: `else-nayak`. Operator-algebraic **completeness** in low `d`: `ogata-h3-index` (`H³(G,𝕋)` index realized/complete in `d=2`), `ogata-classification-review` (`d=1,2` classification). 3+1D commuting-projector realizations from premodular data: `walker-wang`. Exactly solved topological order with anyons: `kitaev-honeycomb`.
- **Homotopy of the space of gapped Hamiltonians / automorphisms (realizing `π₁,π₂,…`):** `aasen-wang-hastings` (conjectural `π₁,π₂,π₃` in 2D; automorphism/honeycomb codes), `hsin-wang-moduli`.
- **No-go / obstruction:** `kapustin-fidkowski` — **no** local commuting projector Hamiltonian realizes nonzero Hall conductance (chiral phases). Chern insulators/IQHE are excluded from this exactly-solvable class.

**(c) Provable today — candidate Theorems.**
- **Thm V-A (Cohomological classes are realized).** Every class in `H^{d+1}(G,U(1))` is realized by an explicit `G`-symmetric commuting-projector lattice model with the prescribed edge anomaly. *(Cite `cglw-cohomology`, `else-nayak`.)*
- **Thm V-B (Completeness in low dimension).** In `d=1,2` the operator-algebraic indices (`ogata-h3-index`, `ogata-classification-review`) are complete invariants of SPT phases with on-site finite symmetry, so realizability is settled there: the invariant set is exactly the realized set.
- **Thm V-C (No-go, exactly).** For local commuting projector Hamiltonians the Hall conductance vanishes; hence chiral invertible classes are **not** realizable in that class. *(Cite `kapustin-fidkowski` verbatim; this bounds any realizability claim.)*

**(d) Open — numbered Conjectures.**
- **Conjecture V-1 (realizability image).** The image of `{uniformly gapped lattice families}/𝓦 → 𝐈𝐏^{cond}_{d,G}` is exactly the **short-range-entangled** sub-spectrum; the non-realized classes form an identifiable obstruction subgroup (containing, in the commuting-projector class, the chiral classes of Thm V-C).
- **Conjecture V-2 (general chiral realizability).** Chiral phases (nonzero chiral central charge / Hall conductance) are realizable by **non-commuting** uniformly gapped local Hamiltonians (true physically for Chern insulators/FQHE) though not by commuting projectors — i.e. the no-go is specific to the commuting-projector class, not to gapped lattice systems.
- **Conjecture V-3 (functorial realizability in disorder).** Realizability is compatible with profinite disorder hulls: a class realized over a point is realized over `Ω` iff a descent obstruction in the relevant condensed cohomology vanishes.

**(e) Key techniques.** Explicit fixed-point/commuting-projector constructions (group-cohomology cocycle models); matrix-product/PEPS parent Hamiltonians; operator-algebraic index construction and the split property (`ogata-*`); anomaly inflow / edge obstruction (`else-nayak`); adiabatic-path homotopy computations (`aasen-wang-hastings`).

**(f) Pitfalls & bounds (READ BEFORE WRITING).**
- **Kapustin–Fidkowski (`kapustin-fidkowski`) bounds every realizability claim.** Never claim commuting-projector realizations of chiral phases. Distinguish "realizable by *some* gapped local Hamiltonian" (broad) from "realizable by a commuting-projector / exactly-solvable model" (obstructed for chiral).
- "Beyond group cohomology" classes (`kapustin-cobordism`) need non-cohomological constructions; do not claim Thm V-A covers them.
- Ogata completeness is for **on-site finite** symmetry in **`d=1,2`**; do not extrapolate to continuous symmetry, higher `d`, or spatial symmetry without the corresponding result.
- `aasen-wang-hastings` homotopy groups of the 2D gapped-Hamiltonian space are **conjectural** — cite as conjecture, not theorem.

## 7. Paper VI — Synthesis (`paperSynthesis`)
### Topological phases of matter in the condensed-mathematics paradigm: a modular research program

**(a) Scope & place.** Compose Parts I–V into the single boxed sequence of §1.9 and exhibit the program as a **modular hierarchy**: each layer takes the previous as input and produces new structure, with emergent properties at each level. The composition is not a monolith — it is five interoperable modules plus a spectrum-level payoff.

**(b) The modular composition (how the papers build on one another).**
1. **Locality (I)** makes `𝓘_{d,G}` a Banach space and the dynamics a condensed morphism → the ground floor `𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}`.
2. **Positivity/`C*` (II)** attaches the condensed observable algebra + compact state space, and — via Aoki — a **solid-`K` invariant**; emergent property: functorial invariants of disordered families over profinite hulls.
3. **Gap (III)** cuts out the (conditionally open, stable) uniformly gapped substack `𝕲𝖆𝖕_{d,G}`; emergent property: a robust `π₀` (phases) once `𝓦` is inverted and ancillas added.
4. **Stabilization/EFT (IV)** group-completes the invertible sector into the connective condensed spectrum `𝐈𝐏^{cond}_{d,G}`; emergent property: higher homotopy = pumps and higher defects, and the relative charge `∂ν` as a spectral boundary map.
5. **Realizability (V)** identifies which spectral classes are physically realized; emergent property: an obstruction subgroup separating abstract classes from lattice-realizable ones.

The transition picture (`U_f`, `Σ_f`, `ν_f`, `∂ν ∈ E^{q+1}(B,U_f)`) is the diagnostic that ties layers 3–5 together; the profinite-disorder thread (`Ω=F^{ℤ^d}`) runs through all five (I: descent of LR; II: crossed products; III: uniform-gap descent; IV: parametrized invariants; V: functorial realizability).

**(c) Provable today vs open.** Provable *within* the program are the module-level theorems (I-A…V-C) and the citation of Aoki/Kubota/Freed–Hopkins/Ogata as the rigorous anchors. Open is the **global** claim that these assemble into a single condensed higher stack with `𝐈𝐏^{cond}` as its invertible spectrum (the union of Conjectures I-1, II-1, III-1, IV-1, V-1). Paper VI states this as the program's **Master Conjecture** and lays out what each part would need.

**(d) Key cross-cutting techniques.** Condensed/solid homological algebra; parametrized stable homotopy; operator-algebraic gapped-phase theory; NCG of disorder. **(e) Global pitfalls.** The two hard walls (CPW undecidability for III; Kapustin–Fidkowski for V) bound the whole program: the synthesis must not claim decidability of the gap or commuting-projector chiral realizations. Noninvertible order requires a condensed *higher* stack (out of current scope).

## 9. Novelty assessment (for the novelty reviewer — record honestly)

**Verdict: the *synthesis* is novel; the *ingredients* are not.** Two mature literatures are being joined for the first time.

1. **Condensed mathematics exists and is mature** (Clausen–Scholze `clausen-scholze-condensed/-analytic/-complex/-analytic-stacks`; independently pyknotic, Barwick–Haine `barwick-haine-pyknotic`), and it already reaches operator `K`-theory: **Aoki's `aoki-solidification`** is the concrete bridge the program leans on (`K_op ≃ Solid(K_alg(𝐴̲))`).

2. **Studying the *space/moduli of gapped systems* and its homotopy type is established** in mathematical physics — but always with **ordinary** topology / smooth sheaves / `C*`-bundles, never condensed:
   - `hsin-wang-moduli` (2023): topology of the moduli space of gapped Hamiltonians (closest precedent; EFT methods).
   - `beaudry-etal` (2024): homotopical foundations of parametrized spin systems (spaces, not condensed objects).
   - `kubota-omega-spectrum` (2025): a rigorous `Ω`-spectrum of invertible gapped spin systems (smooth/operator-algebraic).
   - `denittis-states` (2025) and `denittis-rendel-weyl` (2026): classify phases by homotopy of the space of states of a `C*`-algebra.
   The prospectus itself flags the operator-algebraic `Ω`-spectrum work as "especially close in spirit" — correctly.

3. **No prior work applies condensed / pyknotic / solid machinery to topological phases of matter.** Repeated targeted searches ("condensed mathematics topological phases", "pyknotic quantum matter", "profinite disorder condensed crossed product", "solid K-theory topological insulators") returned condensed math applied elsewhere (e.g. `Whitehead's problem and condensed mathematics`, arXiv:2312.09122) and phases studied by ordinary topology/`K`-theory — but **not the combination**. The specific moves that are new: (i) treating `𝕳𝖆𝖒_{d,G}` as a *condensed* stack with **profinite probes matching disorder hulls** `Ω=F^{ℤ^d}`; (ii) routing invariants through **solidification** (Aoki) rather than ordinary operator `K`-theory; (iii) organizing transitions as a **relative class in generalized cohomology** `E^{q+1}(B,U_f)` inside this condensed environment.

**Honest caveats the reviewer should weigh.** The program does not (yet) prove a new physical invariant beyond existing ones (source §9 concedes this: "not a different Chern number"). Its value proposition is unificatory/organizational — a single category for continuous families, profinite disorder, completions, symmetry, stacking, defects, and transition loci. The genuinely open mathematical content is the Master Conjecture of §7(c) (that everything assembles into a condensed higher stack with `𝐈𝐏^{cond}` its invertible spectrum). Papers must sell the synthesis as a **rigorous program with theorems at the module level and clearly-numbered conjectures at the global level**, never as a completed classification.
